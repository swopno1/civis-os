# CivisOS Roadmap and Vision

## 🌟 The Vision: A Digital Lifeboat for Humanity

In an era of increasing global fragility—whether from climate instability, geopolitical conflict, or the collapse of centralized digital infrastructure—humanity requires a "fail-safe" for coordination.

**CivisOS** is envisioned as an unkillable, decentralized network infrastructure. It is the "Digital Lifeboat" that ensures:
1. **Coordination survives** when the internet goes dark.
2. **Knowledge remains accessible** when centralized servers are unreachable.
3. **Privacy is absolute** when state or corporate surveillance becomes a threat to survival.

Our ultimate goal is to provide a neutral technical ground where survival logistics, community trading, and emergency communication can thrive regardless of external circumstances.

---

## 🗺️ 24-Month High-Level Roadmap

### Phase 1: The Foundation (Months 1-3) - *In Progress*
*Focus: Building the Web-OS environment and core system services.*
- **Objectives:** PWA stability, secure local storage, module sandboxing, and basic permission management.
- **Milestone:** Functional Desktop environment with a "Hello World" module demonstrating OS APIs.

### Phase 2: Mesh & Connectivity (Months 4-7)
*Focus: Enabling peer-to-peer communication without traditional internet.*
- **Objectives:** Full integration of Reticulum Network Stack, Web-Serial bridge for LoRa/ESP32 hardware, and opportunistic syncing.
- **Milestone:** Successful message exchange between two air-gapped devices via LoRa.

### Phase 3: Survival Application Suite (Months 8-12)
*Focus: Developing the core tools needed for crisis management.*
- **Objectives:** Release of "The Big Four" modules:
    - **Chat:** Encrypted P2P messaging.
    - **Vault:** Secure local document storage.
    - **Bulletin:** Decentralized community notice board.
    - **Sense:** Environmental monitoring.
- **Milestone:** Alpha release of the "Survival Suite" for community testing.

### Phase 4: Hardware Sovereignty (Months 13-18)
*Focus: Moving beyond the browser and onto dedicated, resilient hardware.*
- **Objectives:** CivisOS-optimized firmware for ESP32/S3, RISC-V compatibility research, and bootable ISOs for old laptop hardware.
- **Milestone:** First dedicated "Civis Node" hardware prototype.

### Phase 5: Global Resilience & Scaling (Months 19-24)
*Focus: Ensuring the OS can be used by anyone, anywhere.*
- **Objectives:** Radical localization (50+ languages), P2P module distribution (no App Store needed), and "Civis Mesh" global coordination protocols.
- **Milestone:** Deployment in a real-world humanitarian or off-grid pilot program.
